Operating Principles

  • Agents need boundaries: permission scope, tool access, approval rules, and audit evidence are part of the design, not a later control.
  • Strategy must compile: standards, runbooks, wrappers, tests, and deployment paths turn governance into repeatable engineering behavior.
  • Lean teams need leverage: small senior groups can coordinate agents, vendors, and automation when ownership and validation are explicit.
  • Security is operational: secret handling, tenant isolation, credential rotation, publication controls, and review gates are first-class platform primitives.
  • AI is not magic glue: deterministic workflows stay deterministic; agents are used where ambiguity, synthesis, or adaptive execution adds value.

Security Posture

Secrets stay outside git; credentials are scoped by tenant, host, and runtime role; mutating operations require deterministic entrypoints, dry-runs, or explicit approval where impact is high.

Delivery Posture

Work is finished only when there is evidence: tests, build output, browser validation, service status, public route checks, or structured handoff reports depending on the surface.

Leadership Posture

The goal is not larger teams or uncontrolled autonomy. The model is a small accountable group using standards, agents, vendors, and automation with clear ownership of the output.
